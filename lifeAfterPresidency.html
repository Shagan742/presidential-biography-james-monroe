Life After Presidency

After leaving office, Monroe worked on his significant financial debts. He asked for money from the federal government saying he had worked plentifully and deserves this money. He also started working on a book comparing the US government to ancient governments  and then an autobiography, but never completed them. At the University of Virginia, Monroe served on the Board of Visitors. He was the Virginia Constitutional Convention president in 1829, remaining publicly active although retiring from politics. Eventually, Monroe unfortunately died from an illness on July 4, 1831 in New York City.


Fun Fact: Monroe was the third president of the founding fathers to die on the 4th of July.
