Monroe also created the Monroe Doctrine, where the US is neutral to European business and they must not transform one another, instilling peace between the country and continent. However, during his presidency, Monroe was accused of corruption because of winning 2 elections in a row (1816-1820 & 1820-1824). Another controversial moment for Monroe was his neutrality on slavery, especially because he was thought to be owning about 250 slaves. To defend himself, Monroe argued that he would let his slaves die off and not acquire new ones, this was known as “gradual emancipation.”
