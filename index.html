James Monroe

Early Life

James Monroe was born on April 28, 1758 in Westmoreland County, Virginia.Â His parents were Spence Monroe, a great tobacco planter of Scottish descent, and Elizabeth Jones Monroe, who was of Welsh descent. Some of his siblings were James Spence Monroe, Andrew Monroe, and Joseph Monroe. His family owned 500-600 acres in Virginia and he was a decently respected rich kid with ample education.

Growing up, James Monroe went to Campbelltown Academy at 11, run by Reverend Archibald Campbell. At the age of 16, he attended the College of William and Mary before being abruptly interrupted by the necessity of his war effort in the American Revolution. After fighting in a series of battles such as the Battle of Trenton, Battle of Monmouth, Battle of Germantown, and the Battle of Brandywine and eventually getting injured, Monroe quit and got advised by his mentor Thomas Jefferson to study law at that same college. He eventually got a law degree and graduated.
